Triple Triad

Rules

Ascension

The number values of each card are increased by one for every card of that type that is in play.

Descension

The number values of each card are decreased by one for every card of that type that is in play.

Elemental

When an elemental card is placed on a corresponding element, each rank goes up a point. When any card is placed on a non-matching element, each rank goes down a point. This does not affect the Same, Plus and Same Wall rules, where the cards' original ranks apply.

Fallen Ace

Aces becomes susceptible to capture by the lowly 1. If the Reverse rule is also in play, a 1 will then become vulnerable to capture by an ace.

Reverse

The conditions for capturing cards are reversed so that smaller numbers are more powerful than larger numbers.

Plus

When one card is placed touching two others and the ranks touching the cards plus the opposing rank equal the same sum, then both cards are captured.

Same

When a card is placed touching two or more other cards, and the touching sides of each card is the same, then the other two cards are flipped.

Same Wall

The edges of the board are counted as ace ranks for the purposes of the Same rule.

Combo

Of the cards captured by the Same, Same Wall or Plus rule, if they are adjacent to another card whose rank is lower, it is captured as well. This is not a separate rule; any time Same or Plus is in effect, Combo is in effect as well.

Swap

One card from your deck is randomly switched with one of your opponent's. The cards are returned to their original owners at the end of the game.

Chaos

The card you play each turn is selected at random from your deck.
